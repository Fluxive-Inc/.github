### What We Found

Fluxive is an applied AI research lab. We study how to govern systems that think and act on their own — how to manage, contain, and focus autonomous AI so it stays within bounds defined by math, not words.

We found a class of spatial-logical governance patterns that describe how any entity in a system can be bounded and verified using geometry — not policy files, not rules written in natural language, but deterministic math. The patterns map directly to hardware logic gates. And because they are math — not code — they work across any physical substrate.

We've filed patent-pending claims across traditional silicon (CMOS), carbon nanotube FETs, quantum gate architectures, and advanced fab processes. The governance logic doesn't change when the material does.

---

### What We've Proved So Far

**Software:** mForceOS — mForceOS1 (Series 1) is a 7+ module cloud-native governance runtime, live and running on Google Cloud. It allows operators and organizations to define their own boundaries and computes them into deterministic governance logic.

**Hardware:** 13 Verilog RTL modules designed, Ed25519 cryptographic core integrated, 15/15 simulation test suites passing. The patterns work in simulation.

**IP:** 2 Patent Series (S1 — 17 provisionals filed; S2 — 3 expansion provisionals in progress). PCT international filing in progress.

**Published:** *Influence Design in the AI Era* (ISBN 978-1-63742-814-6)

The patterns are showing positive results in software. Now we need to align them to hardware.

### R&D Programs — Taking the Patterns into the Real World

Our research programs fund the next step: fabricating the governance patterns in physical silicon — validating what the simulations and software have already shown.

There is also additional research and commercial opportunity at the software level, where governance patterns create programmable governance surfaces that can be configured, deployed, and updated without modifying the underlying hardware.

**Supply chain:** Design (Saratoga Springs, NY) → Fabrication (domestic foundry) → Assembly (domestic). Zero foreign dependencies.
